About TechRun 2025

Kenya is celebrated worldwide for its dominance in long-distance running, with athletes who exemplify endurance, resilience, and excellence. This spirit of perseverance transcends athletics into the entrepreneurial and innovation ecosystem. Just as marathon runners adapt, strategize, and endure to succeed, tech entrepreneurs and innovators must embrace stamina, resilience, and focus to thrive in the ever-changing digital economy. TechRun is a unique forum that draws on this cultural metaphor showcasing Kenya’s strengths in both athletics and technology. The event positions innovation and entrepreneurship as a “marathon, not a sprint,” emphasizing the long-term vision required to drive impactful innovation and sustainable economic growth.

With 50+ million people, a youthful population full of ambition, and a rapidly growing tech-savvy workforce, Kenya has immense potential to become a new frontier for startups gaining global attention. TechRun serves as a platform to support the growth, impact, and international recognition of the local startup ecosystem, tech talent, and business environment.

Key Features of TechRun 2025

TechRun Innovation Challenge 2025

A youth-centered AI-in-sports competition to encourage creativity, problem-solving, and real-world applications.

AI Showcase & Demo Zone

Interactive exhibitions by startups, corporates, and universities on AI, AIoT, and robotics innovations.

Investor Pitching & Deal Rooms

Dedicated spaces to connect high-potential startups with venture capitalists, angel investors, and accelerators.

AI Policy Roundtables & Panel Discussions

Engaging conversations on AI adoption, digital ethics, data governance, and transformation frameworks.

The TechRun (10KM Run)

A symbolic celebration of endurance, innovation, and community resilience connecting sports, tech, and wellness.

Expected Outcomes

  • Increased visibility of Kenya as a top investment destination for AI and digital transformation.
  • Tangible investor commitments and deals for Kenyan and regional startups.
  • Policy recommendations to strengthen AI adoption and governance frameworks.
  • Youth engagement through competitions, skills development, and innovation exposure.
  • Strengthened ecosystem linkages between academia, startups, corporates, investors, and government.
